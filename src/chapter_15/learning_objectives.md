## Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

1. Explain why standard software quality assurance practices do not transfer to ML model evaluation, and describe what must replace them.
2. Select appropriate evaluation metrics for imbalanced classification problems, and explain the specific failure modes of accuracy in such settings.
3. Interpret a confusion matrix and per-class performance report to identify problems that aggregate metrics conceal.
4. Describe calibration and subgroup evaluation as distinct evaluation requirements with distinct consequences when omitted.
5. Define an evaluation gate as an engineering artefact, and distinguish it from a metric.
6. Explain the roles of model cards, the model registry, and governance review in a production ML system — and articulate who owns each.
7. Apply explainability techniques (Grad-CAM and SHAP) as diagnostic tools during evaluation, and identify what they can and cannot confirm about model behaviour.
