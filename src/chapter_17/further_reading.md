## Further Reading

---

- Liu, N. F., Lin, K., Hewitt, J., Paranjape, A., Bevilacqua, M., Petroni, F., and Liang, P. (2023). Lost in the middle: How language models use long contexts. *Transactions of the Association for Computational Linguistics*, 12, 157–173. arXiv:2307.03172. The empirical study establishing the lost-in-the-middle phenomenon, with controlled experiments across multiple model families and context lengths.

- Lewis, P., Perez, E., Piktus, A., Petroni, F., Karpukhin, V., Goyal, N., Küttler, H., Lewis, M., Yih, W., Rocktäschel, T., Riedel, S., and Kiela, D. (2020). Retrieval-augmented generation for knowledge-intensive NLP tasks. *Advances in Neural Information Processing Systems*, 33, 9459–9474. The foundational paper establishing RAG as an architectural pattern, with formal treatment of the separation of parametric and non-parametric knowledge.

- Karpathy, A. (2017). Software 2.0. *Medium*. The essay naming the Software 2.0 paradigm and articulating the shift from explicit logic to learned weights — the conceptual predecessor to the Software 3.0 framing developed in Chapter 13 of this book.

- Anthropic. (2024). Claude's model specification. *Anthropic Technical Documentation*. The clearest public documentation of how safety classifiers, refusal behaviour, and sycophancy manifest in a production foundation model, with implications for application-level prompt design.

- Wei, J., Wang, X., Schuurmans, D., Bosma, M., Ichter, B., Xia, F., Chi, E., Le, Q., and Zhou, D. (2022). Chain-of-thought prompting elicits reasoning in large language models. *Advances in Neural Information Processing Systems*, 35, 24824–24837. The empirical study establishing chain-of-thought prompting as an effective architectural technique for improving reasoning quality, with cost–quality tradeoff characterisation.

- Perez, E., Huang, S., Song, F., Cai, T., Ring, R., Aslanides, J., Glaese, A., McAleese, N., and Irving, G. (2022). Red teaming language models with language models. arXiv:2202.03286. The systematic methodology for discovering LLM failure modes through adversarial probing, applicable directly to the pre-deployment evaluation of clinical LLM applications.
