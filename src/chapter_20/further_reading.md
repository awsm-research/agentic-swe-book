## Further Reading

- Es, S., James, J., Espinosa-Anke, L., & Schockaert, S. (2023). RAGAS: Automated Evaluation of Retrieval Augmented Generation. *arXiv:2309.15217.* The primary reference for the RAGAS framework: covers the metric definitions, the LLM-based evaluation approach, and the empirical validation of each metric against human judgements.

- Zheng, L., Chiang, W.-L., Sheng, Y., Zhuang, S., Wu, Z., Zhuang, Y., ... & Stoica, I. (2023). Judging LLM-as-a-Judge with MT-Bench and Chatbot Arena. *arXiv:2306.05685.* The foundational empirical study of LLM-as-judge, including the documentation of position bias, verbosity bias, and self-preference, with an analysis of conditions under which LLM judges and human judges agree.

- Papineni, K., Roukos, S., Ward, T., & Zhu, W.-J. (2002). BLEU: A Method for Automatic Evaluation of Machine Translation. *Proceedings of the 40th Annual Meeting of the Association for Computational Linguistics.* The original BLEU paper — worth reading for its explicit acknowledgement of the metric's assumptions and limitations, which have been consistently ignored in the decades since.

- Perez, E., Huang, S., Song, F., Cai, T., Ring, R., Aslanides, J., ... & Irving, G. (2022). Red Teaming Language Models with Language Models. *arXiv:2202.03286.* A systematic treatment of automated red teaming approaches, including the taxonomy of failure categories and the methodology for using LLMs to generate adversarial test cases at scale.

- Gehrmann, S., Clark, E., & Sellam, T. (2023). Repairing the Cracked Foundation: A Survey of Obstacles in Evaluation Practices for Generated Text. *Journal of Artificial Intelligence Research, 77*, 103–166. A comprehensive survey of the structural problems with NLG evaluation, covering reference-based metrics, human evaluation design, and inter-annotator agreement — the most thorough treatment of why standard evaluation practices fail for open-ended generation.

- Weidinger, L., Mellor, J., Rauh, M., Griffin, C., Uesato, J., Huang, P.-S., ... & Gabriel, I. (2021). Ethical and Social Risks of Harm from Language Models. *arXiv:2112.04359.* A taxonomy of LLM harm categories that provides the conceptual foundation for red team threat modelling, particularly relevant for clinical and other high-stakes deployment contexts.
