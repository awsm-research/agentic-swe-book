## Further Reading

---

- European Parliament and Council. (2024). *Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 of the European Parliament and of the Council — Laying Down Harmonised Rules on Artificial Intelligence (Artificial Intelligence Act)*. Official Journal of the European Union, L 2024/1689. Articles 9–17 govern technical requirements for high-risk AI systems; Article 12 specifically mandates logging obligations. Available at https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=OJ:L_202401689

- OWASP Foundation. (2025). *OWASP Top 10 for Large Language Model Applications*. Open Web Application Security Project. The authoritative taxonomy of security vulnerabilities in LLM-based systems, including prompt injection (LLM01), insecure output handling (LLM02), and excessive agency (LLM08) — the three categories most directly relevant to agentic system safety. Available at https://owasp.org/www-project-top-10-for-large-language-model-applications/

- Perez, F., & Ribeiro, I. (2022). *Ignore Previous Prompt: Attack Techniques for Language Models*. arXiv:2211.09527. The original systematic demonstration of prompt injection attacks against deployed language model systems, with specific focus on tool-mediated injection. This is the foundational reference for the attack category described in Section 24.5.3.

- Anthropic. (2023). *Model Card and Evaluations for Claude Models*. Anthropic Technical Report. A practical example of the model card governance artefact — documenting capabilities, limitations, evaluation methodology, and safety testing — that the EU AI Act and equivalent frameworks require of high-risk AI systems.

- Weidinger, L., et al. (2021). Ethical and Social Risks of Harm from Language Models. *arXiv:2112.04359*. DeepMind's comprehensive taxonomy of AI risk categories, covering discrimination, exclusion, information hazards, misinformation, human-computer interaction harms, automation and access harms, and environmental harms. The risk taxonomy that should inform any organisation's AI safety assessment process.

- Seshia, S. A., Sadigh, D., & Sastry, S. S. (2018). Formal Specification for Deep Neural Networks. *Automated Technology for Verification and Analysis*, Lecture Notes in Computer Science, vol. 11138. Springer. The foundational treatment of formal methods applied to neural network safety verification — the direction that the agent evaluation open problem (Section 24.8.1) is likely to develop as the field matures.
