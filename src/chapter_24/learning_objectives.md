## Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

1. Explain why agent safety is a categorically different engineering problem from LLM safety, and identify the specific properties of autonomous action that create this difference.
2. Describe the observability stack required for agentic systems — distributed tracing, structured logging, session replay, and anomaly detection — and explain what each element enables in a post-incident investigation.
3. Explain the purpose and structure of append-only, tamper-evident audit logs, and distinguish them from application logs.
4. Apply four safety constraint engineering patterns — scope restriction, approval workflows, circuit breakers, and sandboxing — to the design of an agentic system.
5. Describe the three principal adversarial threats to agentic systems: goal hijacking, privilege escalation, and prompt injection at tool boundaries.
6. Explain what the EU AI Act requires of high-risk AI systems and what those requirements mean for agentic system architecture.
7. Synthesise the complete SE4AI lifecycle from Chapter 13 and describe how the AgentOps layer closes the loop from data engineering through to governed autonomous action.
